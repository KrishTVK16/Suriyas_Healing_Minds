How to Find Groups

Facilitated Groups: Led by mental health professionals, often through therapy practices or community mental health centers.

Peer-Led Groups: Run by individuals with lived experience, often through organizations like NAMI or DBSA.

Finding Groups: Contact local mental health centers, hospitals, or search online directories. Our practice also offers several facilitated groups.

Note: Both types offer valuable connections and practical strategies for wellness.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is therapy and how does it work?

Therapy is a collaborative process between you and a trained mental health professional. It provides a safe space to explore your thoughts, feelings, and behaviors, and develop strategies to improve your mental health and wellbeing. Different therapeutic approaches are used depending on your needs and goals.

How do I know if I need therapy?

If you're experiencing persistent sadness, anxiety, difficulty coping with daily life, relationship problems, or any mental health concerns that are impacting your quality of life, therapy can help. You don't need to be in crisis to benefit from therapy‚Äîit can be helpful for personal growth and prevention as well.

How long does therapy take?

The duration of therapy varies depending on individual needs, goals, and the type of concerns being addressed. Some people see progress in 8-12 sessions, while others may benefit from longer-term therapy. Your therapist will work with you to determine the best treatment plan.

Is therapy confidential?

Yes, therapy is confidential. Your therapist is bound by professional ethics and legal requirements (HIPAA) to protect your privacy. There are very few exceptions, such as if there's a risk of harm to yourself or others, or in cases of abuse.

What should I expect in my first session?

Your first session is typically an assessment where your therapist will ask about your concerns, history, and goals. You'll also discuss what to expect from therapy and begin to develop a treatment plan. It's normal to feel nervous‚Äîyour therapist will help you feel comfortable.
